Real-time systems must respond quickly to environmental inputs in order to work effectively and safely. Real-time operating systems are a critical part of such systems.

This course exposes students to basic RTOS concepts such as tasks, scheduling algorithms, RTOS customisation, and concurrent real-time programming. By the end of the course, students understand how an RTOS is built and gain practical hands-on experience in customising RTOSs and writing real-time programs.

Prerequisite

Undergraduate students must have completed one of CS2040, CS2040C, or CS2040S with a grade of at least D.

Preclusion

Undergraduate students must not have completed one of CS2106 or CS2271 with a grade of at least D.
