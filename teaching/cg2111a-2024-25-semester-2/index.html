This course is for students who have completed Engineering Principles and Practice I. The project scope extends to challenges in large-scale systems. Students first learn fundamental principles for selected advanced concepts, then design and program a real-world system.

The course involves designing a complex computer engineering system that facilitates information processing and real-world interfacing, while also considering metrics such as scaling, safety, security, sustainability, societal impact, and fault-tolerant design.

Prerequisite

Undergraduate students must have completed one course beginning with CS1010 with a grade of at least D.

Preclusion

Undergraduate students must not have completed CG1112.
